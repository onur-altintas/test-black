You will receive recommendations from the Artificial Intelligence (A.I.) on how many loaves of bread to order for the next day. It is important to understand how BakerAI works when you decide whether to follow its recommendation or not. Therefore, we would like to spend a couple minutes to explain the dynamics of the A.I. recommendation below.

{% if treatment == 'full' %}

BakerAI's algorithm does not have any information about your demand, thus it cannot precisely predict the demand for the next day. However, it chooses the best order quantity by factoring two important elements:

  1. Exploring your demand distribution by either increasing or decreasing the last order quantity
  2. Maximizing your profit by balancing the expected cost of ordering either too many, or too few loaves of bread
    3.
BakerAI will get better at maximizing your cumulative profit, if you follow its recommendation every day.

{% endif %}

How BakerAI software works:

If you were sold-out:
Let's assume that in the evening of Day 6, you ordered 100 loaves to sell on Day 7. You sold all of 100 loaves of bread you ordered on Day 7.

Day Amount
ordered		 Amount
sold		 Amount
leftover
7 100 loaves 100 loaves 0 loaves
Because you were sold out, the A.I. will suggest you to order more for the next day (for Day 8).

If you had some leftovers:
On Day 7, you ordered 100 loaves and you were sold-out. In the evening of Day 7, you decided to order 150 loaves (instead of 100 loaves) for Day 8. On Day 8, you didn't have that much demand for the bread and you could only sell 40 loaves.
Day Amount
ordered		 Amount

8 150 loaves 40 loaves 90 loaves
Because you had some leftovers, BakerAI will recommend you to order less than 150 loaves for Day 9.

The exact order quantity amount will be determined by BakerAI software.
