Instructions:

In this business simulation, you will be the manager of a small bakery. Every evening, as a manager, you order loaves of bread from your supplier to sell them on the next day. The demand for bread is uncertain and it fluctuates independently from day to day. Historically, the demand was as high as 600 loaves of bread a day. Any unsold bread will be thrown away at the end of each day. Your objective is to maximize your cumulative profit for the next {{ total_rounds }} days.

Business Parameters:
Unit selling price (The amount your customers pay you per loaf):   {{ Constants.sale_price | c }}
 Unit purchase cost (The amount you pay your supplier per loaf):   {{ Constants.cost | c }}
Profit Analysis:

You can calculate your profit using the formula below:

(Unit selling price (={{Constants.sale_price| c}}) x Amount sold) - (Unit purchase cost (={{Constants.cost | c}}) x Amount ordered)

The amount sold is determined by the number of loaves you ordered and the demand for that day.
If the demand is less than the amount ordered, you will throw away the unsold bread you already paid for.
Every loaf of bread you throw away costs you {{Constants.cost | c}} (Unit purchase cost).

If the demand is more than the amount ordered, you will miss the opportunity to sell more and make more profit.
For every additional loaf you could have ordered, you would have made additional {{Constants.sale_price |c }} - {{Constants.cost |c }} = 2 E$ (=Unit selling price - Unit purchase cost)
