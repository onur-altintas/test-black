You will receive recommendations from BakerAI on how many loaves of bread to order for the next day. It is important to understand how BakerAI works when you decide whether to follow its recommendation or not. Therefore, we would like to spend a couple minutes to explain the dynamics of BakerAI's recommendation below.

Capabilities of BakerAI

BakerAI's algorithm does not have enough information about your demand distribution, thus it cannot precisely predict the demand for the next day. However, it chooses the best order quantity by factoring two important elements:

  1. Exploring your demand distribution by either increasing or decreasing its previous recommended order quantity
  2. Maximizing your cumulative profit by balancing the expected cost of ordering either too many, or too few loaves of bread
    3.
BakerAI will get better at maximizing your cumulative profit, if you follow its recommendation every day.

How BakerAI makes its recommendations

When BakerAI recommends less than actual demand:
Let's assume that in the evening of Day 6, BakerAI recommended 100 loaves to sell on Day 7. However, the actual demand on Day 7 was 200 loaves .

Day BakerAI's
Advice		 Actual
Demand
7 100 loaves 200 loaves
In this case, BakerAI will increase its recommendation for the next day (for Day 8). The increase amount is calculated using a sophisticated statistical model.

When BakerAI recommends more than actual demand:
For Day 7, BakerAI recommended 100 loaves to order and the actual demand was 200 loaves.
Now for Day 8, BakerAI adjusts its recommendation based on the demand realization and recommends 130 loaves (instead of 100 loaves).
Day BakerAI's
Advice		 Actual
Demand
8 130 loaves 40 loaves
On Day 8, you didn't have that much demand for the bread. Because BakerAI recommended more than the actual demand, it will decrease its previous recommended amount (will recommend less than 130 loaves) using a sophisticated statistical model for Day 9.
