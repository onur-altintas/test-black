The Effect of Information on Complex Decision Making Process

Principal Investigator : Onur Altintas

PhD Candidate in Information Systems at Boston University

Sub-Investigator : Prof. Abraham Seidmann

The Everett W. Lord Distinguished Faculty Scholar
of Information Systems at Boston University

Purpose:
The purpose of this study is to investigate the effect of information in a complex business setting.

Duration:
If you decide to take part in this study, you will be asked to make decisions in a simulation that will take about 30 to 50 minutes.

Risks and Benefits:
There is no physical risk in this study. You can skip any of the questions you do not want to answer. We will not collect and store any directly identifiable information. All of the information we collect will be stored in a secure manner and only study team members will have access to it. There are no expected risks or benefits in this study.

Payment:
You will be paid {{ participation_pay }} USD for completing the study. There will be no cost to you to participate in this study. Also, you will get additional payment based on your performance. The monetary amounts represented in the study will be experimental dollars (denoted by E$). At the conclusion of the study, the experimental dollars you earn will be converted to dollars at a rate of {{ rate }} E$ per $1 US dollar (USD).

Your participation in this study is completely voluntary. You are free not to participate or to withdraw at any time, for whatever reason.
